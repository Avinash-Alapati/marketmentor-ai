# Futures Contracts

## Definition

A futures contract is a standardized agreement to buy or sell an asset at a predetermined price on a future date.

Futures are traded on exchanges and are commonly used for hedging and speculation.

---

## Features

- Standardized contracts
- Margin trading
- Daily mark-to-market settlement
- Expiry date

---

## Example

Suppose NIFTY Futures trade at 25,000.

If you expect the index to rise, you can buy a futures contract.

If the price increases to 25,300, your position gains value.

---

## Advantages

- Leverage
- Hedging
- High liquidity

---

## Risks

- High leverage increases losses
- Margin calls
- Volatile price movements

---

## Beginner Mistakes

- Trading with excessive leverage
- Ignoring risk management
- Holding contracts until expiry without understanding settlement

## Key Takeaway

Futures are powerful financial instruments that should be used with proper risk management and understanding of leverage.
