# Support and Resistance

## Definition

Support and Resistance are price levels where buying or selling pressure tends to increase.

Support acts as a floor.

Resistance acts as a ceiling.

---

## Support

A support level is an area where buyers are expected to enter the market.

Price often bounces upward from support.

---

## Resistance

A resistance level is an area where sellers are expected to enter the market.

Price often reverses downward from resistance.

---

## Breakout

When price breaks above resistance with strong volume.

Often signals continuation.

---

## Breakdown

When price breaks below support.

Often signals bearish continuation.

---

## Advantages

- Easy to identify
- Useful for entries
- Useful for stop-loss placement

---

## Limitations

- Levels are zones, not exact prices
- Fake breakouts occur frequently

---

## Beginner Mistakes

- Treating levels as exact lines
- Ignoring volume
- Ignoring higher timeframe structure

---

## Related Topics

- EMA
- MACD
- RSI

---

## Key Takeaway

Support and Resistance are foundational concepts in technical analysis and should always be viewed as zones rather than exact prices.
