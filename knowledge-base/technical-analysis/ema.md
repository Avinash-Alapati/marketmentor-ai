# Exponential Moving Average (EMA)

## Definition

The Exponential Moving Average (EMA) is a trend-following technical indicator that gives more importance to recent price movements than older prices.

Because it reacts faster to recent market changes, traders often use EMA to identify trends and potential entry or exit points.

---

## Formula

EMA = (Current Price × Multiplier) + (Previous EMA × (1 − Multiplier))

Multiplier = 2 ÷ (Period + 1)

---

## Common EMA Periods

- 9 EMA
- 20 EMA
- 50 EMA
- 100 EMA
- 200 EMA

---

## Interpretation

### Price Above EMA

- Bullish trend
- Buyers are in control

### Price Below EMA

- Bearish trend
- Sellers are in control

---

## EMA Crossover

A bullish crossover occurs when a short-term EMA crosses above a long-term EMA.

Example:

9 EMA crossing above 21 EMA.

A bearish crossover occurs when the short-term EMA crosses below the long-term EMA.

---

## Advantages

- Reacts quickly to price changes
- Helps identify trend direction
- Easy to understand

---

## Limitations

- Generates false signals in sideways markets
- Lagging indicator
- Should be combined with price action

---

## Beginner Mistakes

- Trading every crossover
- Ignoring higher timeframe trend
- Using EMA without confirmation

## Key Takeaway

EMA is one of the most widely used trend indicators. It should always be combined with market structure and risk management.
