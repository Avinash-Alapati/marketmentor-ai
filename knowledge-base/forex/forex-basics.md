# Forex Market Basics

## Definition

Forex (Foreign Exchange) is the global marketplace where currencies are bought and sold.

It is the largest financial market in the world, with trillions of dollars traded every day.

Unlike stock markets, Forex operates 24 hours a day during weekdays.

---

## Major Currency Pairs

Examples:

- EUR/USD
- GBP/USD
- USD/JPY
- AUD/USD
- USD/CAD

These pairs involve the world's most actively traded currencies.

---

## Base Currency

The first currency in a pair.

Example:

EUR/USD

Base Currency = EUR

Quote Currency = USD

---

## Pip

A pip is the smallest standard movement in a currency pair.

Example

EUR/USD moves from

1.1000

to

1.1001

Movement = 1 Pip

---

## Lot Size

Standard Lot = 100,000 Units

Mini Lot = 10,000 Units

Micro Lot = 1,000 Units

Nano Lot = 100 Units

---

## Leverage

Leverage allows traders to control larger positions with smaller capital.

Example:

Leverage 1:100

₹10,000 capital controls ₹10,00,000 worth of currency.

Higher leverage increases both profits and losses.

---

## Spread

The spread is the difference between:

Bid Price

Ask Price

Lower spreads generally mean lower trading costs.

---

## Advantages

- High liquidity
- 24-hour market
- Low transaction costs
- Easy access through brokers

---

## Risks

- High volatility
- High leverage risk
- Economic news impact
- Emotional trading

---

## Beginner Mistakes

- Using excessive leverage
- Ignoring risk management
- Trading during major news without preparation
- Overtrading

---

## Related Topics

- Futures

---

## Key Takeaway

Forex trading offers significant opportunities but also carries substantial risk. Successful traders focus on risk management, discipline, and continuous learning rather than quick profits.
