# Options Trading Basics

## Definition

An option is a financial contract that gives the buyer the right, but not the obligation, to buy or sell an underlying asset at a predetermined price before or on a specified date.

---

## Types of Options

### Call Option

Gives the buyer the right to buy an asset.

Used when expecting prices to rise.

---

### Put Option

Gives the buyer the right to sell an asset.

Used when expecting prices to fall.

---

## Important Terms

- Strike Price
- Premium
- Expiry Date
- Intrinsic Value
- Time Value

---

## Example

Suppose NIFTY is trading at 25,000.

You buy a 25,200 Call Option expecting the market to rise.

If NIFTY moves above 25,200 before expiry, the option may gain value.

---

## Advantages

- Limited risk for option buyers
- Leverage
- Hedging opportunities

---

## Risks

- Time decay
- Volatility
- Premium loss
- Complexity for beginners

---

## Beginner Mistakes

- Buying options without understanding time decay
- Ignoring implied volatility
- Overtrading

## Key Takeaway

Options are versatile financial instruments that can be used for speculation, income generation, and hedging, but they require a solid understanding of pricing, risk, and strategy.
