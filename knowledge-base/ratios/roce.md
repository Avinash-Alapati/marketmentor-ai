# Return on Capital Employed (ROCE)

## Definition

Return on Capital Employed (ROCE) measures how efficiently a company generates profits from the total capital invested in the business.

Unlike ROE, ROCE considers both shareholders' equity and long-term debt, making it useful for evaluating companies with significant borrowings.

---

## Formula

ROCE = EBIT / Capital Employed × 100

Where:

- EBIT = Earnings Before Interest and Taxes
- Capital Employed = Total Assets − Current Liabilities

---

## Example

Suppose:

- EBIT = ₹250 Crore
- Capital Employed = ₹1,250 Crore

ROCE = (250 ÷ 1250) × 100

ROCE = 20%

This means the company generates ₹20 operating profit for every ₹100 of capital employed.

---

## Interpretation

### High ROCE

- Efficient use of capital
- Strong operational performance
- Healthy profitability

### Low ROCE

- Inefficient use of capital
- Poor operational efficiency
- Weak returns on investments

---

## Advantages

- Measures operational efficiency
- Useful for comparing capital-intensive businesses
- Considers debt along with equity
- Preferred metric for manufacturing companies

---

## Limitations

- Not suitable for comparing different industries
- Can be affected by one-time gains
- Historical value may not represent future performance

---

## Practical Example

Company A

ROCE = 24%

Company B

ROCE = 14%

Company A is generating more operating profit from its capital base.

---

## Beginner Mistakes

- Confusing ROCE with ROE
- Ignoring debt while evaluating companies
- Comparing companies from unrelated sectors
