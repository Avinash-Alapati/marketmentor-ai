# Price-to-Book (PB) Ratio

## Definition

The Price-to-Book (PB) Ratio compares the market price of a company's share with its book value per share.

It indicates how much investors are paying compared to the company's net asset value.

---

## Formula

PB Ratio = Market Price per Share / Book Value per Share

---

## Example

Suppose:

- Market Price = ₹300
- Book Value = ₹150

PB Ratio = 300 ÷ 150 = 2

Investors are paying ₹2 for every ₹1 of the company's net assets.

---

## Interpretation

### PB > 1

The company is trading above its book value.

This often indicates investors expect future growth.

---

### PB < 1

The company is trading below its book value.

Possible reasons:

- Undervalued company
- Poor financial performance
- Weak investor confidence

---

## Advantages

- Useful for banking and financial companies
- Helps identify undervalued stocks
- Easy to calculate

---

## Limitations

- Less useful for technology companies
- Ignores intangible assets
- Book value may not reflect current market value

---

## Practical Example

Company X

- Market Price = ₹500
- Book Value = ₹250

PB = 2

Company Y

- Market Price = ₹500
- Book Value = ₹100

PB = 5

Company X is cheaper relative to its assets.

---

## Beginner Mistakes

- Comparing companies from different sectors
- Ignoring debt levels
- Using PB alone
