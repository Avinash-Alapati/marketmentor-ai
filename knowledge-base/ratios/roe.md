# Return on Equity (ROE)

## Definition

Return on Equity (ROE) measures how efficiently a company uses shareholders' equity to generate profits.

It is one of the most important profitability ratios.

---

## Formula

ROE = Net Profit / Shareholders' Equity × 100

---

## Example

Suppose:

- Net Profit = ₹100 Crore
- Shareholders' Equity = ₹500 Crore

ROE = (100 / 500) × 100 = 20%

The company generates ₹20 profit for every ₹100 invested by shareholders.

---

## Interpretation

### High ROE

Usually indicates:

- Efficient management
- Strong profitability
- Better utilization of shareholder funds

---

### Low ROE

May indicate:

- Poor profitability
- Inefficient capital allocation
- Weak business performance

---

## Advantages

- Measures management efficiency
- Useful for comparing companies in the same sector
- Popular among long-term investors

---

## Limitations

- High debt can artificially increase ROE
- Should not be used alone
- Industry averages vary significantly

---

## Practical Example

Company A

- ROE = 24%

Company B

- ROE = 11%

Company A is generally generating higher returns on shareholder capital.

---

## Beginner Mistakes

- Ignoring debt levels
- Comparing companies from different industries
- Looking only at one year's ROE
